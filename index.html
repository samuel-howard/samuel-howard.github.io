I am a First Year DPhil student in the StatML CDT programme, based in the Department of Statistics at the University of Oxford. My current research is focused on reinforcement learning with high-dimensional data and lower dimensional structure, and is supervised by Patrick Rebeschini and Ciara Pike-Burke.

Previously, I obtained my Masters in Mathematics at Oxford, where I focused on Functional Analysis and Probability Theory.

MMath Dissertation
Best-of-Both-Worlds Bandits: An Introduction and Extension of the Tsallis-INF Algorithm
Nov 2021 – May 2022
  • Supervised by Patrick Rebeschini.
  • Conducted a literature review of the Tsallis-INF algorithm, which achieves optimality in both stochastic and adversarial bandit structures.
  • Proved regret bounds for a novel extension of the algorithm to the case of Bandits with Paid Observations.

Jun 2021 – Aug 2021 Oxford Mathematical Institute, Data Science Group
  • Supervised by Samuel Cohen and Tanut Treetanthiploet.
  • Studied and implemented the novel ARC multi-armed bandit algorithm.
  • Adapted the algorithm to contextual bandits, and implemented the result using Python in TensorFlow’s TF-Agents library.
  • Compared performance against existing algorithms in a variety of standard and novel bandit structures.
